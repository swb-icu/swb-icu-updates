Clinical Updates
🌡️ Post-Cardiac Arrest Guideline — Temperature Target Update The post-cardiac arrest guideline is currently under review following discussions with neurophysiology colleagues. Updated international guidance issued last year has revised temperature management recommendations. The key change is a shift to avoidance of pyrexia, with a revised target temperature threshold of 37.5°C (previously 37.7°C). This is a minor but important update and will be incorporated into the updated guideline shortly.
📋 Brain Stem Testing — CMO Guidance On 3 February 2026, the Chief Medical Officers of England, Scotland, Wales and Northern Ireland jointly wrote to Medical Directors and clinical leaders across the UK regarding the updated 2025 Academy of Medical Royal Colleges Code of Practice for the Diagnosis and Confirmation of Death. The letter provides a clear and unequivocal endorsement of neurological death testing as good clinical practice and calls for its consistent application in all appropriate cases, regardless of donation potential. All hospitals are required to ensure local policy, education, and clinical practice fully reflect the Code. The four CMOs have collectively confirmed their support for clinicians applying it — including in situations where the diagnosis is questioned. Read the FICM summary →

⚠️ Learning from Safety Events — Misplaced CVC Line The SALG Patient Safety Update (March–June 2025) includes a case directly relevant to ICU practice and worth reading carefully.

A young adult admitted for orthopaedic surgery became unwell in theatre and was treated for suspected anaphylaxis. Due to persistent hypotension, an ultrasound-guided right internal jugular central line was inserted. The line was used for inotropes and vasopressors. The patient did not wake when sedation was stopped. CT head demonstrated widespread infarction. When the CVC was transduced approximately 48 hours later, an arterial trace was found (carotid). The patient was subsequently confirmed dead by neurological criteria and went on to organ donation.

Key learning: TRANSDUCE BEFORE USE.

Ultrasound does not guarantee correct placement. In haemodynamically compromised patients, arteries and veins may be difficult to distinguish. The vein may be transfixed by the needle or guidewire. Consideration should be given to using a cannula to facilitate transduction and exclude arterial placement before dilatation. Checklists are recommended, especially in emergency situations. Extubation Safety Checklist
Introduced April 2026 · Laminated copies at every bedside

The extubation safety checklist was introduced within Critical Care on 3 April 2026 as part of a quality improvement initiative aimed at supporting structured assessment, improving MDT communication, and reducing variability around extubation practice.

The workflow has two parts: an initial ASSESS section to support review and optimisation of extubation readiness, followed by the checklist itself as a cognitive aid during the extubation process and post-extubation monitoring.

The checklist has been refined through MDT feedback, governance discussions, and consultant review, with input from nursing staff, ACCPs, physiotherapists, and governance colleagues. Work was presented at the Governance Meeting and Divisional Surgical Safety Meeting. Engagement across the MDT has been excellent, with positive feedback on usability and shared situational awareness during high-risk extubations.

An audit is now underway to assess rates of failed extubations and documentation pre and post-checklist introduction. Results to follow.

🫁 Russell PneumoFix — Chest Decompression Device The Russell PneumoFix is a sterile chest decompression device for the management of tension pneumothorax. It is a catheter device with a low-pressure release valve, inserted over a Veress-tipped needle. These are stocked within the ICU emergency trolley and should be used in preference to a Venflon for this purpose.

If you may need to decompress a tension pneumothorax, please familiarise yourself with the procedure using these short training videos: Video 1 →   Video 2 →
